A4C PACS-Dataset for Echocardiographic Phase Detection

  • November 9, 2021
  • By Beth Lane

Acquisition of high-quality, ethically approved medical imaging datasets for the training and testing of Deep Learning networks remains a challenge due to several contributing factors. These include, but are not limited to, time and cost constraints of both researchers and health service professionals. Moreover, a lack of dataset sharing amongst the research community makes the direct comparison of published results difficult.

My 2021 study titled “Multibeat Echocardiographic Phase Detection Using Deep Neural Networks” presents an automated model capable of identifying multiple end-systolic and end-diastolic frames in apical four chamber view (A4C) echocardiographic videos of arbitrary length. The network performance on multi-centre data is indistinguishable from that of human experts, with significantly shorter processing time.

Three datasets were used in this study, 1 for training & testing and the others for testing only so as to assess the generalisability of the architecture.

The datasets are:

  • PACS-dataset: 1,000 videos aquired using Philips Healthcare (iE33 xMATRIX) from NHS Trust PACS Archives, Imperial College Healthcare, London
  • MultiBeat-dataset: 40 videos of 10 beats in length aquired using GE Healthcare (Vivid.i) and Philips Healthcare (iE33 xMATRIX) from St Mary’s Hospital, London
  • EchoNet-dataset: 10,030 videos aquired using Siemens Healthineers (Acuson SC2000) and Philips Healthcare (iE33, Epiq 5G, Epiq 7C) publicly available from Stanford University Hospital
